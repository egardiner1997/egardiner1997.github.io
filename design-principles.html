Activity 02: Design Principles

Visual Hiearchy

Facebook.com

https://www.facebook.com/

Visual Hiearchy is where certain things are either different sizes, colors, or shapes to draw the attention of your audience. Facebook is an example of visual hiearchy because the timeline is the main focus of the website. Then you have the icons on the top that are the next biggest thing on the page to show that you should click them next explore. Some headlines on ads or posts are bigger than others because they want to draw your attention.

Repetition

Wells Fargo

https://www.wellsfargo.com/

Repetition is the repeating of certain fonts, colors, shapes, and placements. In the case of web design it can also be related to link and the way they are set up. Wells Fargo's online site has drop down boxes repeated at the top of the page and when you go down the page the links are also placed in similar places. They also repeat a lot of the same color scheme and box shapes.

Proximity

Deviantart.com

https://www.deviantart.com/

Deviantart is an example of proximity because they fill up spaces with images so that there is very little blank space. They leave enough space to show the differences in size with the images, but they love to fit the boxes together to make sure there isn't any negative space.
